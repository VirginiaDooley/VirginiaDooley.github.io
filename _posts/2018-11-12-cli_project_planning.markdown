As the end of OO Ruby drew near, I could see CLI projects just around the corner. The thought of creating something "from scratch" was too exciting. At this stage, I felt I had a reasonable understanding and command of the scraping concepts required for this project. My biggest risk would be getting carried away with something too complex. 

I decided to head over to the #womenintech channel on http://slack.learn.co and solicit some advice from a few good women who had recently completed the project. "Keep it simple" was the main take away. Sound advice for someone who likes to day dream about all the things I will build one day! The other lesson learned was to make sure that I found the the subject matter of the project interesting. With this in mind, I jotted down a few ideas: 

1. An ethical holiday shopping guide
2. A winter sun finder
3. Income inequality by borough
4. A podcast finder

Next, I conducted a few searches for possible websites to scrape. Using www.builtwith.com, I made sure the sites were not too Javascript heavy. And with information in mind, the decision was made! I'd love to build all of these gems once time allows, but for now it's back to my CLI project: "PodcastMe". Stay tuned for the result!
