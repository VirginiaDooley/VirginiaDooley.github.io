[Learning to code is a lesson in resiliency](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XLeUvZvuvAs )

"I can't do it." This is a phrase my kids mutter 99 times a day.  With the typical optimism parents need to get their little ones through the early years, I reply with: "You can't do it...YET, but you will get it."

After working in the often messy international development sector, I realised that technology was the medium I needed to find new inspiration to catalyze change. On the brink of parent hood, I wanted to find flexibility in my career to spend time with my family as needed. 

I've managed to get the basics on my own, but ultimately I realized the need for accountability and support offered with Flat Iron School to finally bridge my learning gap. In what feels like a very long coding journey so far, I'm reminded to practice what I preach and keep at it (and ask for help!).
